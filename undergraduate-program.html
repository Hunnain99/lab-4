Bachelor of Science in Information Systems

Preparing students for exciting careers in the IS field.

About the Program

The B.S. in Information Systems program prepares students for all potential career positions in the IS field, including Systems Analysis and Design, Computer Networking, and Health Care Informatics.

For successful completion of the degree, all students must satisfy the general education requirements outlined in the UMBC Undergraduate Catalog.

Below is a detailed list of the current required courses for the Bachelor of Science Degree. Course descriptions can be found in the Course Catalog.

Current Required Courses

Information Systems

  • IS 300 – Management Information Systems
  • IS 310 – Software and Hardware Concepts
  • IS 410 – Introduction to Database Design
  • IS 420 – Database Application Development
  • IS 425 – Decision Support Systems
  • IS 436 – Structured Systems Analysis & Design
  • IS 450 – Data Communications and Networks
  • IS 451 – Network Design and Management
  • IS 3xx or 4xx – Upper-Level IS Elective

Mathematics and Computer Science

  • MATH 155 – Applied Calculus
  • MATH 215 – Finite Mathematics for Information Sciences
  • STAT 351 – Applied Statistics for Business and Economics
  • IS 147 or CMSC 201 – Computer Programming I
  • IS 247 or CMSC 202 – Computer Programming II
  • One additional approved programming course (e.g., IS246, IS413)

Administrative Science

  • MGMT 210 – The Practice of Management
  • ECON 101 – Principles of Microeconomics
  • ECON 102 – Principles of Macroeconomics
  • ECON 121 – Principles of Accounting I
  • ECON 122 – Principles of Accounting II
  • ENGL 393 or IS 369 – Technical Communication

Prerequisite Structure Chart

Note: Students must earn a grade of “C” or better for any course to be applied to the Information Systems major. For more details, consult the Course Catalog.

Important Notes

  • Before taking IS 410 and IS 450, students must complete the following gateway requirements:
    • A grade of “C” or higher in MATH 155 and IS 147
    • A grade of “B” or higher in IS 300 and IS 310
  • IS 300 and IS 310 must be completed in residence at UMBC.
  • Special courses (e.g., IS 399, IS 478, IS 469) cannot be used to fulfill upper-level elective requirements but offer valuable experience for internships and research projects.
